Digital Colonialism in Google's Workforce Redistribution: A South African Perspective

In this essay I explore the digital colonialism through the lens of Google's recent workforce shifts, which saw hundreds of jobs moved to countries like India and Mexico. This practice mirrors historical colonial exploitation, creating economic dependency and job insecurity in the Global South. Focusing on South Africa, I highlights the challenges faced by local developers and calls for awareness, fair labor practices, and leveraging local strengths to foster a more equitable tech industry. The essay advocates for collective action and global solidarity to combat the exploitative dynamics of digital colonialism.
